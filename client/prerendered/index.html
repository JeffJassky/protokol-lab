01 · Dose tracking

See how much is
still in your system.

Every dose has a half-life. Protokol Lab plots the active amount in real time so you know exactly how much medication is working right now — and why your appetite comes and goes the way it does. One-tap presets for Tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound), Semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus), Liraglutide, and Dulaglutide. Compounded versions of those peptides, or anything else you need to track, add as a custom compound in two fields: a name and a half-life. The curve draws itself.

02 · Weekly calories

Off day? It's fine.
Look at your week.

An off day isn't a broken streak. GLP-1s flatten your hunger for a couple days, then it comes back — and a 24-hour calorie counter treats that like failure. Your target here is a 7-day total: eat light when you can't eat, catch up when you can.

  • Unused calories carry forward · they don't disappear at midnight
  • Built for uneven weeks · suppression days are expected, not penalized
  • Today, in context of the week · every macro tells you exactly how much is left for today to stay on pace

03 · Agentic AI

An assistant that
does the work.

Not a chatbot. Snap a photo of your plate or just tell it what you ate — the AI identifies the food, checks your library, searches common nutrition databases, falls back to a web search, and writes the entry into your day. It also knows every dose and symptom you've logged, so its advice is grounded in your data, not generic GLP-1 talking points.

Show it. Tell it.
It logs it.

Most trackers give you a chat window and call it AI. This one actually does things. Snap a meal photo or describe what you ate — it identifies the items, looks them up across your library and common nutrition databases, falls back to a web search if it has to, and writes the entries into today's log. Then it tells you what you've got left to hit your targets.

  • Show it your plate · photo → identification → macros → log entry, end to end
  • Looks everywhere · your library first, then common nutrition databases, then web search as fallback
  • Knows your context · every food, dose, weigh-in, symptom, and note is available — every answer is grounded in your data
  • Hits your numbers · "340 kcal and 80g protein to go" — and a list of foods from your library that get you there
  • Reads symptoms with doses · why the nausea is hitting on day 2, why your hunger comes back day 4
