About

Built by GLP-1 users
for GLP-1 users.

Protokol Lab exists because generic calorie counters don't understand what life on a GLP-1 actually looks like. They treat every day as independent. They have no concept of dose timing, of pharmacokinetic half-lives, or of the weekly appetite pattern that defines life on tirzepatide or semaglutide. We built the tracker we wanted.

Why this tracker exists

A 24-hour calorie target assumes roughly consistent daily appetite. GLP-1 medications break that assumption. Appetite suppression peaks in the first 48–72 hours after a weekly injection, then fades as the drug's serum level drops. Traditional trackers flag low-appetite days as "under goal" and rebound days as overage — turning normal GLP-1 physiology into perceived failure. Protokol Lab is built around that cycle from the first principle: weekly rolling budgets, not midnight resets; pharmacokinetic curves, not static numbers.

What we do

  • Model every dose with the Bateman equation so users see the active level in their system in real time, not just the injection schedule.
  • Treat calories as a 7-day budget that rolls forward, so a low-appetite day after the shot feeds capacity into the days when appetite returns.
  • Track symptoms on a 0–10 severity scale with correlation against the dose curve, so nausea and side effects become predictable patterns rather than random bad luck.
  • Ship an agentic AI that reads your full log — food, dose, weight, symptoms — and writes entries for you from plain-English descriptions.

What we're not

  • We are not a telehealth provider. We do not prescribe, sell, ship, or refer users to pharmacies or compounding facilities for any medication or substance.
  • We are not a medical device. Protokol Lab is an organizational and mathematical modeling tool. It does not diagnose, treat, or recommend doses.
  • We do not publish pharmacokinetic defaults for anything that is not FDA-approved. Users who track custom compounds supply their own values.
  • We do not sell, share, or monetize user health data. We never will.
