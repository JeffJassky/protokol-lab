# Founder Interview Questions

Goal: extract first-person material that no LLM can fabricate, to upgrade the 5 existing posts and source the 8 new ones. Questions are organized by article. Answer in any format — voice memo, bullet notes, full prose, screenshots — whatever's lowest-friction.

The most valuable answers will be:
- Specific dates, doses, weeks, weights, calorie counts
- Concrete moments ("I was standing in the kitchen at 9pm and…")
- Numbers from your own logs / charts
- Things you tried that *didn't* work, in order
- Mainstream advice that pissed you off
- Things your prescriber, psych, or community got wrong

You don't need to answer every question. Skip ones that don't have a story attached. The point is to get raw material, not to fill a form.

---

## 0. Foundational background (informs every post)

These set the founder voice for the whole blog. We can pull from them anywhere.

1. ADHD diagnosis: what age, what type (inattentive / hyperactive / combined), how long had you suspected before getting diagnosed?
2. On stimulants? Which one, what dose, how long, any history of switching?
3. Weight history: lifelong struggle, adult-onset, post-something specific? Highest weight, current weight, goal weight (or "the goal stopped mattering once X happened")?
4. List every diet/tracking app you've abandoned, and how long you lasted on each before quitting. Honest count.
5. The specific failure pattern: do you abandon when the app gets too tedious, when you have a bad weigh-in, when life gets busy, all of the above?
6. When did you start GLP-1? Which compound, what starting dose, what current dose, on label or compounded, switched formulations or not?
7. The moment you decided to build this app instead of using existing ones. What broke that day?
8. Software background: what do you build for a living, how technical are you with PK / stats / data viz?
9. How long have you been dogfooding the app on yourself? What's the feature you use every single day vs the feature you almost never look at?
10. Who has seen your data? Prescriber, psych, partner, nobody? Any reactions worth quoting?

## How to use this doc

For each article we draft, the writer (me, in subsequent sessions) will pull the relevant question block from this doc and your answers into a working draft. Answers can be raw — full sentences not required. Voice memo transcripts are great. Screenshots can replace 200 words.

If a question doesn't have a story attached for you, skip it. If a question reminds you of a story I didn't ask about, write it down anyway. The unprompted ones tend to be the best material.
